Know the warning signs

When should you see a cardiologist?

Don't ignore these symptoms — early evaluation can save your life.

  • Chest pain, pressure or tightness
  • Breathlessness on exertion or at rest
  • Palpitations or irregular heartbeat
  • Dizziness, fainting or blackouts
  • Swelling in the legs or ankles
  • Family history of heart disease
  • High blood pressure, diabetes or high cholesterol
  • Unusual fatigue or reduced exercise capacity

Patient information

Frequently asked questions

How do I book an appointment?

You can call 097434 63444 during clinic hours, or use the appointment form below. Our team will confirm a convenient slot with you.

What should I bring to my first consultation?

Please bring any previous medical records, ECG/echo reports, a list of your current medications, and details of any allergies. This helps Dr. Koregol give you the most accurate advice.

Do you treat emergencies?

For a medical emergency such as severe chest pain, please call 108 or go to the nearest emergency department immediately. This website is not for emergency care.

Is angioplasty a major surgery?

Angioplasty is a minimally invasive, catheter-based procedure — not open-heart surgery. Most patients are awake during the procedure and recover quickly. Dr. Koregol will explain what to expect in your case.

How often should I have a heart check-up?

Adults over 40, or anyone with risk factors like diabetes, high blood pressure, smoking or a family history of heart disease, should consider an annual cardiac evaluation. Dr. Koregol can advise the right schedule for you.
